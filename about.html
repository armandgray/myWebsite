The Story

Armand Gray is a San Francisco native living in Oakland, CA. Armand had the unique privilege of attending a full French emersion school growing up and attend a top college, Pomona, in Southern California. Joining Teach for America after graduation from Pomona and taking on a role as a basketball coach, Armand spent the next two years exploring the possibilities of a teaching career. Armand just completed an M.A. in Urban Education through Loyola Marymount University. But, at the moment, Armand is making a career shift from his current position as a Math Teacher in the Oakland Unified School District.

The Work

In May of 2016, Armand Gray took his first steps into exploring computer science by leaning on his college network to learn about strong potential resources to investigate. Immediately finding his new passion, Armand was able to find a more full resource when he joined the Mission Techies program at the beginning of summer '16. Through the Mission Techies coding program, Armand was able to join Dev Mission where he would spend the next 6 months diving into all things Android as he develops his first project management & communication app.
